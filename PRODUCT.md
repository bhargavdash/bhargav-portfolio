# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, engineering leads, and senior/staff frontend engineers evaluating
Bhargav Dash for a stronger Frontend or Full-Stack role. They arrive from a LinkedIn
DM, a job application, or a referral — usually skimming on a laptop, occasionally on
mobile, with a stack of other tabs open. They have ~30 seconds to decide whether this
person is worth a call. The job to be done: quickly judge engineering credibility,
range (React / React Native / MFE architecture / hand-rolled AI systems), and taste —
then find the proof (live projects, resume, contact).

## Product Purpose

A single-page portfolio that converts a cold skim into "let's talk." It exists to
land Bhargav a better role within ~12 months. Success = the visitor leaves with a
concrete next action taken (resume opened, project visited, or contact made) and a
durable impression of someone senior who sweats the details. The site itself is the
strongest work sample: the craft of the page IS the argument for the candidate.

## Brand Personality

Bold and memorable — but disciplined, never loud. Three words: **confident,
precise, alive.** The motion, the custom cursor, the dark indigo palette should read
as deliberate engineering taste, not decoration. Voice is direct and technical
without jargon-flexing; it shows rather than tells. The page should feel like it was
built by someone who could build the product you're hiring for.

## Anti-references

- **Generic dev template** — the cookie-cutter "hero + three icon cards + skills bars
  + contact form" starter. No identity, interchangeable with 10,000 others.
- **Over-animated demo reel** — motion for its own sake; everything floating,
  parallaxing, bouncing. Bold must come from confidence and distinctiveness, not from
  piling on effects. Every animation earns its place or it's cut.
- **Corporate / agency SaaS** — cold gradient-and-glass B2B marketing look that could
  belong to any company. There must be a human behind this.
- **Cluttered maximalism** — competing focal points, no breathing room. Bold ≠ busy;
  the boldness lives in a few committed moves surrounded by space.

## Design Principles

1. **The page is the proof.** Every detail is a demonstration of engineering craft.
   A misaligned baseline or a janky scroll undermines the entire pitch.
2. **Bold through commitment, not accumulation.** A few decisive moves (the palette,
   the display type, one signature interaction) carry the personality. Resist adding
   more; resist toning down what's there.
3. **Motion with intent.** Animation guides attention and rewards interaction. If a
   motion doesn't serve the read or the delight, it's cut. Always reduced-motion safe.
4. **Show, don't tell.** Demonstrate range with real projects and live work, not
   adjective lists. Let the artifacts do the talking.
5. **Skimmable in 30 seconds, rewarding on a second pass.** Hierarchy delivers the
   pitch instantly; craft details reward the visitor who lingers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (≥3:1 large), visible keyboard focus indicators
(never removed without replacement), logical tab order, semantic landmarks, and full
`prefers-reduced-motion` support across the custom motion system (smooth scroll,
cursor, reveals, count-ups, tilt). Touch targets ≥44px on mobile.
